Summary of Our Project

The overall national business cycle could be shown in many different things. Based on our assumption,a slump economy would have following characteristics:

1. fewer busiensses operate

2. unemployment rate increase

3. wage decreases

4. spending decreases

5. tax decreases etc.

A boom would face opposite phenomenon. In our project, we would only focus on the previous two factors that is the number of operating businesses and the unemployment rate. We use stock price as an indicator of the national business cycle, and the number of business license application as the indicator for number of operating businesses. We choose the city of Chicago as a sample representing the overall economic behavior of United State, and with data analysis, we try to figure out whether the two factors have the relationships with the national business cycle as what we assumed. However, based on the result of the regression, we find that there is no significant relationship between stock price and license application

Summary of Our Findings

As most of people will assume when the stock market price goes up indicates the economy is good, therefore the number of business license application should also increase. However, in our regression analysis we found the opposite relationship

We found significant negative relationship between HIGH stock market price and the number of license application. Which means when the stock market goes up, there is less business license application.

We also found significant positive relationship between LOW stock market price and the number of license application. Which means when the stock market goes down, there is more business license application.

This finding is totally different than what we imagined, therefore we can expect when the stock market is not doing well, people change their behavior to open more businesses, therefore we observe more business license application when the stock market price goes down.
