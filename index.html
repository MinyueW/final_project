Summary of Our Project

The overall national business cycle could be shown in many different things. Based on our assumption,a slump economy would have following characteristics:

1. fewer busiensses operate

2. unemployment rate increase

3. wage decreases

4. spending decreases

5. tax decreases etc.

A boom would face opposite phenomenon. In our project, we would only focus on the previous two factors that is the number of operating businesses and the unemployment rate. We use stock price as an indicator of the national business cycle, and the number of business license application as the indicator for number of operating businesses. We choose the city of Chicago as a sample representing the overall economic behavior of United State, and with data analysis, we try to figure out whether the two factors have the relationships with the national business cycle as what we assumed. However, based on the result of the regression, we find that there is significant relationship between stock price and license application

Multiple Data Analyzation

Business License and Stock Market

Business license data and stock market data have significant relationship both in recent 15 years and 5 years.

For the fifteen years analysis, we found there is a positive relationship between business license application and stock market price both in the high price and low price. When the stock market price change, the business license application also changes by 0.0016. From the p-value we can see there is statistical significant relationship, but the coefficient is very small, thus when we plot the relationship with regression line, we cannot observe much angle on the regression line.

The result is similar for the recent 5 years.

As most of people will assume when the stock market price goes up indicates the economy is good, therefore the number of business license application should also increase. This assumption is confirmed by our analysis, though the small coefficient indicate the influence is not strong

We found significant positive relationship between HIGH stock market price and the number of license application. Which means when the stock market goes up, there is more business license application.

We also found significant positive relationship between LOW stock market price and the number of license application. Which means when the stock market goes up, there is more business license application.

This finding verifies the our assumption on the relationship of business cycle and number of businesses. When the economic condition is good, people are more likely to invest.

Unemployment rate and Stock Market

Unemployment and stock price have significant negative relationship both in fifteen years and five years analysis.

As the high stock price goes up, the unemployment rate goes down.

As the low stock price goes up, the unemployment rate goes down.

This is same as what we predicted, when the stock market is doing good, and the stock price goes up, we can conclude the economy is doing well at the same time. Therefore, when economy environment is good, businesses are more likely to invest more, thus to hire more employees, and unemployment rate goes down. We plotted the regression, and we see clearly angle in the graph, and shows the stock market price goes up, the unemployment rate goes down.

Business License and Unemployment Rate

We found NO significant relationship between unemployment rate and business license application.

We used to think while the business license application increase, means employers will hire more employees, therefore when business license application goes up, we should observe unemployment rate goes down. However, our analysis tells us differently, business license application has no effect on the unemployment rate.

First of all, the number of bussinesses though goes up with upward sloping economy, the influence is not strong. Further, one possible fact is that many of the businesses are in really small size and the requirement for staff is not high enough to change the unemployment rate.

GIS Map Analysis

We graph the number of license application with geographic map of Chicago. We colored it from white to dark red to represent the amount of business license application from minimum 56 to maximum 439.

We assumed the highest number of business application area should be at Chicago downtown or the loop area. However, we found something different.

The Chicago downtown area is only light pink colored, which indicates this area the license applications are less than 200, compare to the 439 as maximum. Further more, the area surrounding Chicago downtown are all white colored, including the south loop area is also nearly white colored, which means, these areas are the lowest business license application area.

The highest application area, the only dark red area, is the area nearby South lake shore drive, and right in front of Hyde Park. This area is the Oakland and Kenwood area, including Graveland Park, Ellis Park, Chamberlain Triangle Park, and Kenwood Park.

Therefore, we conclude probably because of the limited space and high rent price in downtown area, business in recent years are moving to the area outside of downtown Chicago, we observe more business open in areas other than downtown Chicago.
